[h2] You’re here because you want to feel better, eat well, exercise consistently, and take good care of your body.
[p] Yet it is hard to know where to start.
[p] Do you need to count calories or go on a diet?
[p] Do you need to invest in fancy gym membership?
[p] How do you change your habits while balancing multiple demands of work, family, and relationships?
[p] You’re asking all the right questions.
[p] Instead of trying to figure it out on your own…
[p] What if you had atrusty guide and proven processto get better health and wellbeing?

[p] If you keep eating poorly, skimping on sleep, and neglect your body…
[p] Your life will always seem out of balance.
[p] The rollercoaster of stress and shame will continue.
[p] You need to take responsibility for evolving yourself.
[p] You need a new approach to care of your body, mind, and heart.
[p] Here’s the rub:
[p] You cannot look at health in a vacuum and disregard the social and emotional context of your life.
[p] You need to see yourself as a collection of interconnected parts and understand how all these elements—genetics predispositions, personal preferences, work demands, family responsibilities, and cultural constraints—impact your behavior.
[p] This is why I adopt a holistic systems-level approach when working with all my clients.
[p] 
[h2] Men’s Life Coaching: 4 Pillars. 1 Goal
[p] I’m offering you a way to align yourself internally and externally so you can achieve amazing results.
[p] My main goal is to help you create deep well-being based upon the truth of who you are—a man ready to step into his full power.
[p] One-on-one coaching sessions help you achieve your full potential and give you accountability for fulfilling it.
[p] We will address your eating habits, your exercise routine (or lack thereof), your coping mechanisms for dealing with stress, your mindsets, and so much more.
[p] I use meta-framework that can hold the complexity of who you are without forcing you into predetermined “Man Boxes” that limit your potential.
[p] Personalized men’s life coaching helps you get unstuck without short-term unsustainable diets, self-sabotaging feedback loops, or unwanted side effects.

[h2] The 4 Pillars of the (Man)ual Upgrade
[p] 
[a] Watch the Video
[h3] Waking Up:Cultivate awareness to understand the root cause of what’s not working in your life.
[li] 
[li] Is your diet, sleep, or exercise out of whack?
[li] Are you being honest with yourself?
[li] Are you willing to feel emotions that seem intolerable?
[p] 
[h3] Cleaning Up:Make peace with the past, address your unmet needs, and let go of emotional baggage.
[li] 
[li] Where have you internalized beliefs that you are not enough?
[li] Who and what do you need to forgive?
[li] What do you need to release to give you the energy to move forward?
[p] 
[h3] Growing Up: Build the skills you need to become the person you want to be.
[li] 
[li] How would the person you would like to be, do the things you’d like to do?
[li] What mindset would you need to accomplish your goals?
[li] What skills do you need to learn to achieve your dreams?
[p] 
[h3] Showing Up:Live in the present moment as an emotionally mature, powerful, purpose-driven man.
[li] 
[li] Make the best possible decision with the tools you have available.
[li] Balance strength and vulnerability with a dose of self-compassion.
[li] Respect yourself and honor the person you are becoming.
[h2] The 1 Goal of the Men’s Life Coaching
[p] No, the goal of men’s life coaching is not cool-looking headstands (although we can work on that too.)
[h3] The Goal of Men’s Life Coaching Is…Freedom!
[li] Freedom to use your body without thinking twice about whether you can handle it.
[li] Freedom to be unshackled by the unrealistic or outdated expectations of others.
[li] Freedom to have autonomy and control over your own destiny.
[li] Freedom to eat however you like while taking full responsibility for the consequences on your health.
[li] Freedom to look at yourself in the mirror and feel proud, strong, and appreciative of who you are.
[li] Freedom to express yourself authentically in a body you have grown to appreciate and respect.
