[h1] My Story
[a] Watch The Video
[h3] I knew things were out of balance because I was…
[li] Making the same stupid decisions over and over.
[li] Feeling bloated, moody, and generally weak.
[li] Uncomfortable taking my shirt off.
[li] Struggling with feeling like no matter what I did, it was never enough.
[li] Pushing harder than ever before with diet and exercise with little results.
[li] Bouncing between “good” days and “terrible” days.
[li] Lacking the energy or confidence I once had.
[li] Taking on too many obligations and experiencing anxiety and burnout.
[li] Getting in my own way with perfectionism and self-judgment.
[li] Feeling ashamed about my struggle but couldn’t talk about any of it.
[h2] I Know What It’s Like To Feel Frustrated & Stuck In A Body You Don’t Like
[h2] I battled an eating disorder when I was a teenager.
[p] I was plagued by voices in my head — obsessed with
[a] counting calories,
[a] what I could eat, and how I could exercise to
[a] manipulate my body.
[p] I stopped eating altogether because starving myself was safer than experiencing the intense and complex emotions that I felt in my body.
[p] The struggle with myself became too much to bear. Moreover, it was impacting all those around me: friends and family I cared about.
[p] I needed to change. For myself. For others.
[p] I hit an all-time low when I was crying on the sofa at my parent’s house. I realized that I wanted to live a healthy life but I couldn’t do it on my own.
[h2] Asking for help is not giving up; It’s growing up.
[p] I surrendered the need to control every aspect of my world and allowed help into my life.
[p] I dropped out of high school, got hospitalized, and began the long journey of nurturing myself back to health with the support of medical professionals, psychologists, nutrition experts, and more.
[p] It was the hard work of turning self-criticism into self-care, every day.
[p] I knew I was the only one who could determine the outcome of this battle. I knew it was my responsibility to take care of my body.
[p] I also knew the way we’ve all been taught
[a] how to take care of ourselves is woefully inadequate.
[p] I had to empower myself and relearn to trust my body so I could rely on it when life really got tough.
[h2] The Challenge of Building Sustainable Strength
[p] As I continued down my path of healing, I began to question the assumptions on which my education was built.
[li] Is my crappy mood just a neurotransmitter imbalance?
[li] Is my body just a calorie-burning machine?
[li] Are my beliefs even really “mine” or the conditioning I received?
[p] I realized there’s a big hole in the western biomedical approach to health and wellbeing. It disconnects the mind and body. It treats everything as a machine to be programmed, manipulated, and drugged. It is incomplete and reductionist.
[p] I’m totally in favor of Western medicine to treat acute diseases. But when it comes to chronic ailments – body pain, diabetes, obesity, heart disease, anxiety, burnout, etc. – we need a more comprehensive approach.
[p] Lifestyle is medicine. Food is medicine. Sleep and social support are medicine.
[p] I wanted a more comprehensive grounding to inform everything science had to say about healthy living. As a result, I was drawn to the eastern spiritual traditions during my time living in Malaysia and Hong Kong (where I spent 4 years studying Buddhism at the University of Hong Kong).
[p] The gift I received was access to ancient wisdom on living well.
[p] My Masters in Mind & Brain Education at Harvard University allowed me to further integrate the technology of mind and body — applying biology, psychology, and eastern spirituality to health, fitness, and personal development.
[p] I wanted a framework that was built on a solid foundation of a lifelong practice that results in sustainable wellness. This meant integrating the best of East and West, science and spirituality, mind and body.
[p] The old stories and methods were no longer adequate. I had to redefine what it means to be a healthy and highly evolved man in today’s world.
[p] I want to help you do the same.
